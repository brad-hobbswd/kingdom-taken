CHAPTER FOUR

The Meeting in the Ruins

Weeks passed after the first stirrings began. Elias could no longer ignore the pull drawing him beyond familiar streets and routines.

City lights flashed. Music drifted through the evening air. Crowds moved between restaurants and storefronts. Something deeper demanded his attention.

The pull guided him through narrow streets and into an abandoned industrial district. Broken windows reflected distant lights. Steel structures leaned beneath years of neglect. Silence settled over the forgotten place.

Yet the atmosphere felt alive. Expectation filled the air.

Near shattered concrete and rusted beams stood another man. Matthias looked up from scattered notes and worn manuscripts. Recognition passed between them immediately. Not familiarity. Something deeper.

Neither understood fully. Both knew they had been brought there.

Footsteps echoed from nearby shadows. Abram emerged from darkness carrying his flashlight. His eyes moved between both men. No surprise crossed his face. Only understanding.

Stories unfolded. Pressure. Stirrings. Strange encounters. Strength arriving after speaking the name Jesus.

Separate experiences suddenly formed one larger picture. None of them had been called alone.

Something had begun moving through the city. Heaven had started gathering watchmen.

Three paths had finally become one.
